Computer Hardware

The physical components of a computer, such as its chassis, random access memory (RAM), CPU, monitor, mouse, keyboard, computer data storage, graphics card, sound card, speakers, and motherboard, are referred to as computer hardware.  Software, on the other hand, is a collection of instructions that may be stored and executed by hardware. Software is referred to as "soft" because it is flexible, but hardware is referred to as "hard" because it is rigid in terms of modifications. Software often instructs hardware to carry out any command or instruction. A useful computer system is one that combines hardware and software, while there are also systems that use simply hardware.

Case

The majority of the system's parts are enclosed by the computer case. It controls and directs the flow of cooling air across internal components and offers mechanical support and protection for internal components including the motherboard, disc drives, and power supplies. The enclosure also functions as a component of the system to regulate the electromagnetic interference the computer emits and safeguards interior components from electrostatic discharge. While desktop cases offer less expansion capability, large tower cases typically stand on the floor and can accommodate several disc drives or other accessories. A video display is included into all-in-one design cases. Computers that are portable or laptop-sized need casings that protect them from impacts. Hobbyists could use paint, colourful lights, or other features, in an activity called case modding.

Power supply

Alternating current (AC) electric power is transformed into low-voltage direct current (DC) electricity for the computer by a power supply unit, or PSU. A built-in rechargeable battery can power laptops.  Power MOSFETs (power metal-oxide semiconductor field-effect transistors) are commonly utilised in the converters and regulator circuits of the switched-mode power supply (SMPS), which is what the PSU typically employs.

Motherboard

A computer's motherboard is its primary part. It is a board with integrated circuitry that connects the CPU, RAM, disc drives (CD, DVD, hard disc, or any others), and any peripherals attached via ports or expansion slots. A typical computer's integrated circuit (IC) chip houses billions of tiny metal-oxide semiconductor field-effect transistors (MOSFETs).

Storage devices

Any computational gear or digital medium used for storing, transferring, and extracting data files and objects is referred to as a storage device. It can be internal or external to a computer, server, or other comparable computing device and contain and store data both transiently and permanently. A key feature and essential part of computers is data storage.

Input device

Users can enter data into systems or manage their functioning using input devices. A mouse and keyboard are standard on personal computers, although touchpads are frequently used on laptops in place of mice. Webcams, microphones, joysticks, and image scanners are among additional input devices.

Output device

The senses of people are considered when designing output devices. For instance, monitors show text that is readable and speakers create audible sound. [12] Other examples of such gadgets include printers and Braille embossers.
